Vegetable Biryani is a fragrant Indian rice dish made by layering spiced vegetables and partially cooked basmati rice, then slow-cooking them together to meld the flavors.

    Ingredients
  • Rice: 1-2 Cup
  • oil: 1-2 tbsp
  • Vegetables
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Potato
  • Spices

Steps:-

  1. Par-cook the Rice:-

    2. Boil 5 cups of water with whole spices and salt. Add soaked rice and cook until it is 75% done (it should still have a slight bite). Drain and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Vegetable Gravy:-
    • Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Sauté half the sliced onions until golden brown; remove and set aside for garnishing.
    • In the remaining oil, sauté the rest of the onions and ginger-garlic paste until fragrant.
    • Cook until vegetables are fork-tender but not mushy. The gravy should be thick, not watery.
  3. Layering (Dum Process):-

    4. In a heavy pot, spread half the vegetable gravy.Top with half the par-cooked rice.Sprinkle fried onions, mint, and coriander.Repeat the layers, finishing with rice on top. Drizzle saffron milk and a spoonful of ghee.

  4. Final Cooking:-

    5. Seal the pot tightly with a lid (using foil or a damp cloth to trap steam). Cook on the lowest heat for 15–20 minutes. If using a thin-bottomed pan, place it on a preheated tawa (griddle) to prevent burning.Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening. Fluff gently with a fork and serve with Raita or Salat.
